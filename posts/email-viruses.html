Emails and Viruses

Emails are the number one way viruses are passed on to computers, in fact, it is the attachment to the email that includes the malware. Don't worry, there is good news! There are simple things users can do to protect their computer from these harmful attachments. The most important thing is to look at what the file extension is. File Extensions are the three letters after a period in the attachment name. A few examples of safe file extensions are .JPG, .JPEG, .TIFF, and .MPG, .MPEG, .MP3, and .WAV. If you get an email with an attachment whose file extension has two three letter sets (something.axe.exe) be very careful, these typically have malware!
